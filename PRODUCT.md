# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: PC customization enthusiasts — the Wallpaper Engine / Rainmeter crowd — who love the Wii aesthetic and want their desktop to feel personal and alive. Secondary: everyday users who just want a prettier, simpler way to launch their apps and games. Both are at their own PC, in a relaxed context; the launcher runs for long sessions in the background of daily life, so it must delight without demanding attention or resources.

## Product Purpose

Wee is a Nintendo Wii-inspired desktop launcher for Windows (Electron). It turns the desktop into a living, customizable channel grid for launching apps, games, and websites, with wallpapers, sounds, widgets, and deep per-surface personalization. Success, in priority order: it becomes the user's daily launcher (they keep it running and launch everything through it); it becomes a canvas for self-expression (crafting and sharing presets, themes, and setups); and opening it delivers the small joy of turning on a console.

## Positioning

The most playful, personality-rich launcher on PC: a fully customizable desktop canvas — channels, widgets, sounds, wallpapers — with console feel and desktop power. It is not a faithful Wii recreation; it takes heavy inspiration from the Wii, modernizes and matures it while keeping its heart, and leans into what only a PC can do (admin quick actions, multi-monitor, deep configuration).

## Brand Personality

Playful, polished, nostalgic — and underneath that: whimsical, premium, alive; friendly, fluid, crafted. The interface should feel like a well-made toy for adults: bouncy and warm in its motion and sound, but precise and confident in its craft. Reference feels: Wii/Switch system menus (the bounce, the sounds, the physicality of channels), Apple's fluid springs, glass materials, and interruptible gestures, and Wallpaper Engine's deep customization that never overwhelms.

## Anti-references

Not generic SaaS or dashboard chrome — no sterile grids, corporate grays, or admin-panel blandness. Not Windows utility clutter — no dense settings trees, no system-tray-app energy. Playful should never collapse into either extreme: sterile tool or noisy gadget.

## Design Principles

1. **The feel is the feature.** Motion, sound, and tactility are core product value, not decoration — but every animation must convey state or personality without blocking the task.
2. **Modernize the heart, not the pixels.** Channel Wii's warmth and physicality through modern materials (glass, springs, soft light); never pixel-perfect cosplay.
3. **Depth without overwhelm.** Deep customization revealed progressively; the first-run experience is simple, the power is discoverable.
4. **Built for all-day sessions.** It runs constantly, so restraint is craft: gate heavy effects, minimize churn, never hog resources.
5. **One vocabulary of whimsy.** A single motion and design system (shared springs, tokens, primitives) carries the personality — no one-off flourishes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect OS reduced-motion everywhere as a hard rule (shortened or snapped transitions, no lingering animated trees). Target WCAG AA contrast for all text and controls. Core flows (launching channels, navigating pages, settings) must be fully keyboard navigable.
